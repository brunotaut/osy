Key UX/UI patterns
Behavior-based risk scoring — "loitering over runway threshold, risk: HIGH" is far more actionable than "drone detected at lat/lon." The score is calculated from intent signals, not just presence.
Single queue, risk-prioritized — operator sees one ordered list of threats ranked by Risk Intelligence score. They work top-to-bottom, never triage a raw alert flood.
Defeat Manager in-UI — jammer controls appear in the same interface without switching apps or screens. The loop closes where the operator is already looking.
Auto PTZ priority queue — cameras are assigned to targets automatically in order of Risk Score. Operator sees what matters first; camera resources are never wasted on low-risk tracks.
Alert consolidation — multiple detections of the same drone auto-group into one Event. Prevents the same threat flooding the alert list as separate entries from different sensors.
Apply to our system
Build threat scoring from day one — not just "drone detected" but a Risk Score based on proximity to protected zones, flight pattern (loitering vs. transiting), speed, and sensor confidence.
Present targets as a single risk-ordered queue, not a flat event log. The operator's first action should always be on the highest-priority item.
Interceptor dispatch controls should be inline with the target — not a separate panel the operator has to navigate to after identifying the threat.
Key lesson for our system
Dedrone's biggest insight is that detection without context is noise. Their Risk Intelligence feature — scoring by flight behavior, not just position — is the right model for our sensor fusion output. An alert that says "loitering 180m from runway threshold, Risk: 94" is immediately actionable. An alert that says "drone at 51.4°N 0.2°W" is not. Design our threat scoring layer around intent signals from day one.

Unified UX/UI Takeaways

Principles appearing across 3+ solutions — industry-validated patterns our system must implement.
01
Dark theme is the universal standard
Every solution uses dark UI. Operators work in low-light rooms; radar sweeps need contrast; video feeds need neutral surroundings. Dark = less eye fatigue, better video legibility, clearer threat color coding.
All 11 solutions
02
Map is the primary input device
Not just a display. Target selection, camera tasking, drone routing, zone definition — all initiated by map tap. No coordinate text fields in primary workflows. The map is where decisions happen.
AMC · DroneSentry-C2 · Elysion · VSD-C2
03
Single consolidated Actions panel
All operator responses in one place. When a threat appears, the operator must not hunt for the button. DroneSentry-C2 rebuilt their entire UI around this single insight in v10.
DroneSentry-C2 · Elysion · XTEND XOS
04
Operator-on-the-loop, not in-the-loop
AI handles detection, camera slewing, and tracking by default. The operator confirms, overrides, or escalates — not approves every sub-task. Cuts reaction time dramatically.
Walaris · Elysion · DroneSentry-C2
05
Threat confidence always visible
Every detected target shows classification status (Confirmed / Probable / Unverified) and confidence level. The system communicates its own certainty. Prevents acting on false positives.
Walaris · DroneSentry-C2 · Elysion
06
Operational mode always declared
Calibration / Monitor / Operational — always in the top bar. The operator must never be uncertain whether the system is actively scanning. Mode switching = one click, never buried in a menu.
DroneSentry-C2 · Elysion · AMC
07
Mission abstraction over platform control
Operator commands missions ("intercept target"), not platforms ("drone A, fly to coordinate X"). System resolves intent into device commands. The right model for interceptor dispatch.
XTEND XOS · Elysion · DroneSentry-C2
08
Layout presets, not free-form windows
2–3 fixed layouts tied to operational phases (Monitor / Verify / Intercept). Layout changes with mode automatically. Never ask operators to manage window arrangement during an incident.
Eyeson · DroneSentry-C2 · VSD-C2
09
Pre-launch safety gate
Before interceptor dispatch: auto-check GPS lock, battery, target confidence, airspace clear. Visual go/no-go. Block launch until all pass. Force-override requires explicit confirmation step.
Auterion AMC · Elysion
10
Stream quality always declared
Every video pane shows quality: 🟢 HD / 🟡 SD / 🔴 Degraded / ⬛ Lost. Silent failure is the worst outcome — operator may act on a frozen or stale frame. Quality is never implied.
Eyeson · DroneSentry-C2
11
Persistent device strip
Always-visible strip at screen bottom. One tile per active device (drone, camera, sensor): battery, signal, state. Tile turns red on fault. No navigating menus to check device health.
XTEND XOS · AMC · DroneSentry-C2
12
One fused target, not raw sensor events
Operator sees one target object with merged data from radar + RF + acoustic + camera. System fuses; operator decides. Showing raw sensor data to operators creates confusion and slows response.
Elysion · DroneSentry-C2 · Walaris · Aaronia

— What to avoid

Anti-Patterns Observed in Competitive Solutions

UX failures documented across 11 systems — patterns our system must not replicate.
✕
Free-form window management. Cambridge Pixel VSD allows unlimited customisation. Under stress, operators waste time on window arrangement. Our system locks layouts to 3 presets that change automatically with operational mode.
✕
Raw multi-sensor data exposed to the operator. Separate radar, RF, and acoustic events per sensor forces mental fusion under time pressure. Our system always fuses server-side; operator sees one object per threat.
✕
Video on a separate screen from the map. If the operator must navigate away from the map to see the camera feed, they lose positional context. Our system keeps video embedded in the same view at all times.
✕
Silent video degradation. A frozen or low-quality frame with no indicator is worse than no feed — the operator may act on stale information. Our system always declares stream quality explicitly on every feed.
✕
Alerts without decision prompts. "Drone detected" with no guided next action forces operators to recall workflow under pressure. Every alert in our system suggests a next action and is framed as a decision, not a notification.
✕
Platform-level commands instead of mission-level. Requiring operators to select a specific drone and enter coordinates manually is error-prone and slow. Our system abstracts to "Intercept target X" — the backend resolves the assignment.
✕
Exposing raw RF/spectrum data to operators. Aaronia's RTSA-Suite PRO is an excellent tool for RF engineers, not operators. Our system consumes expert sensor data and surfaces only the meaningful result: position, confidence, classification.
✕
Replay as video-only playback. Incident review must be an investigation workflow with a scrubable event timeline, sensor confidence history, and operator action log — not just pressing play on a recording. Design this from day one.
